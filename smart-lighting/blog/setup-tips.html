Smart Lighting Setup Tips for Beginners

Just got your first smart bulb or LED strip? Here's how to set it up for the best experience — from naming conventions to automation routines.

1. Start Small

Don't try to smart-fy your entire home at once. Start with one room — the living room or bedroom — and add more as you get comfortable.

2. Use Logical Naming

Name your bulbs by room and position — e.g., "Living Room Main," "Kitchen Counter," "Bedroom Lamp." This makes voice commands much easier: "Alexa, turn off Living Room Main."

3. Create Routines

Most smart lighting apps let you create schedules. Set a "Good Morning" routine that gradually brightens your bedroom lights, or a "Good Night" scene that dims everything to warm white and turns off after 30 minutes.

4. Experiment with Scenes

Scenes let you set multiple lights to specific colours and brightness at once. Create a "Movie Time" scene that dims the main light and turns on the TV backlight in blue.

5. Use Motion Sensors

Motion sensors are the secret to truly hands-free lighting. Place one in the hallway or bathroom, and set it to turn on lights for 2 minutes after detecting movement.

Quick Tips

  • Group lights by room for easier control
  • Use "away from home" schedules while on holiday
  • Update firmware regularly for new features
  • Start with Wi-Fi bulbs, then add a hub for reliability
