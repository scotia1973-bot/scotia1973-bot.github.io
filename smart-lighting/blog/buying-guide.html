The Complete Smart Lighting Buying Guide

Smart lighting is one of the easiest and most rewarding smart home upgrades. Whether you want voice-controlled bulbs, automated motion sensors, or immersive TV backlighting, this guide covers everything you need to know.

What Is Smart Lighting?

Smart lighting refers to light bulbs, LED strips, switches, and sensors that can be controlled remotely via smartphone app, voice assistant (Alexa, Google Home, Siri), or automation schedule. Unlike traditional lighting, smart lights let you change brightness, colour, and even colour temperature on the fly.

Key Features to Look For

Connectivity

Wi-Fi bulbs connect directly to your router — no hub required. Great for beginners and small setups. Examples: TP-Link Tapo, Govee Wi-Fi bulbs.

Zigbee/Thread bulbs need a hub (like Philips Hue Bridge or Amazon Echo Plus) but are more reliable and responsive in larger setups.

Colour vs White

RGB (colour) bulbs let you choose any colour from 16 million hues — ideal for mood lighting and parties. Tunable white bulbs let you adjust from warm (2700K) to cool (6500K) white — better for reading or focused task lighting.

Brightness

Look for bulbs with 800+ lumens for main room lighting. Dimmable bulbs give you more control over ambiance.

Ecosystem Comparison

Philips Hue is the most popular ecosystem with the widest range of bulbs, strips, sensors, and accessories. Works with all major voice assistants.

Govee offers excellent value LED strips and TV backlighting with vibrant colours and music sync. Their app is one of the best.

IKEA TRÅDFRI is the budget-friendly option with solid Zigbee bulbs and smart blinds. Works with Hue hub via "Friends of Hue."

Nanoleaf leads with Matter/Thread support and unique panel designs for wall art.

Our Recommendations

For most people, start with a Philips Hue Starter Kit (4 bulbs + hub) for the most reliable experience. Add Govee LED strips for accent lighting and a Hue Motion Sensor for automated hallways.

On a budget? TP-Link Tapo L530E bulbs at £12.99 each need no hub and work with Alexa and Google Home.

Key Takeaways

  • Wi-Fi bulbs are cheapest and easiest for small setups
  • Zigbee/Thread hubs give better reliability for whole-home systems
  • Start with one ecosystem to avoid fragmentation
  • Motion sensors and schedules save energy and add convenience
