The Complete Portable Projector Buying Guide

Portable projectors have come a long way. Today's models pack 1080p resolution, Android TV, built-in batteries, and auto-focus into a device the size of a coffee cup. Here's what to look for.

Brightness (Lumens)

Brightness is the most important spec. Measured in ANSI lumens (standardised) or LED lumens (manufacturer-rated):

  • Under 200 lumens: Only usable in complete darkness
  • 200–500 lumens: Good for dark rooms and evening outdoor use
  • 500–1000 lumens: Works with some ambient light
  • 1000+ lumens: Usable in daylight with curtains drawn

Resolution

1080p (Full HD) is the sweet spot for portable projectors. Native 1080p offers crisp, detailed images. Some budget projectors claim "1080p support" but natively output 480p or 720p — always check the native resolution. 4K portable projectors exist (BenQ GP500, JMGO N1 Ultra) but cost significantly more.

Battery Life

If you plan to use your projector outdoors or without a power socket, battery life is critical. Most portable projectors offer 1.5–3 hours of playback. The Anker Nebula Capsule 3 offers 2.5 hours, while the Kodak Luma 350 has 1.5 hours. USB-C Power Delivery support means you can recharge with a power bank.

Built-in Smart TV

Projectors with built-in Android TV, webOS, or Fire TV don't need an external streaming stick. You can run Netflix, Disney+, YouTube, and Prime Video directly. The XGIMI MoGo 2 Pro and Samsung Freestyle 2 both have excellent smart TV interfaces.

Auto Features

Modern portable projectors include auto focus, auto keystone correction, and auto screen alignment. These features save significant setup time, especially when moving the projector between rooms or taking it outdoors.

Key Takeaways

  • Prioritise native 1080p resolution for clarity
  • 300+ lumens minimum for any real use
  • Battery projectors are convenient but dimmer than AC-powered ones
  • Android TV built-in saves carrying a separate streaming stick
