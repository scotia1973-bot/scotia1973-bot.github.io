Programmers click, scroll, and navigate more than almost any other profession. An ergonomic mouse isn't just about comfort — it's about preventing repetitive strain injuries that can end careers.

For Programmers Without Wrist Issues: MX Master 3S

The sculpted shape, horizontal scroll wheel (perfect for long code lines), and thumb gesture button make this the most productive mouse for coding. Read our full {amz_link(ASINS['mx3s'], 'MX Master 3S review')}.

For Wrist Pain Prevention: Vertical Mouse

Vertical mice rotate your hand into a handshake position, reducing pressure on the carpal tunnel. The Logitech Lift is our top pick for its comfortable shape, quiet clicks, and excellent build quality. Available in left and right-handed versions.

For Minimal Desk Movement: Trackball

Trackball mice never need to move — your thumb or finger rolls the ball while your hand stays stationary. The Kensington Expert Mouse Wireless with its 54mm ball is the gold standard, especially once you install KensingtonWorks for gesture customization.
