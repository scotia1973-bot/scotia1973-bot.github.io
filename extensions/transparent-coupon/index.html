How We Make Money (No Secrets)

💚 The Transparent Coupon Model

When you click a deal or product link through our extension and make a purchase, the store pays us a small commission (typically 1-10% of the sale). This does not increase the price you pay.

What We Do:

  • Add our own affiliate tag to shopping links
  • Show our earnings in the Transparency Dashboard
  • Open source all our code for public auditing

What We NEVER Do:

  • Hijack or replace existing affiliate links on any page
  • Sell your browsing data to anyone
  • Show paid placements without disclosure
  • Use dark patterns or deceptive UI
  • Hide better deals from you

This isn't just marketing — it's engineered into our extension. Our code is open source on GitHub. Anyone can verify exactly what we do.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this really free forever?
Yes, 100% free forever. We make money through affiliate commissions when you shop through deal links — you never pay a cent. There are no premium tiers, no subscriptions, and no paid features.
Wait... what's the catch with "affiliate commissions"?
No catch. When you click a product or deal link in our extension and buy something, the store pays us a small commission (typically 1-10%). This does NOT increase the price you pay — you'd pay the same amount whether you used our link or went directly to the store. It's the same model as Wirecutter, RTINGS, and most honest review sites.
Do you hijack affiliate links like Honey did?
Absolutely not. We never modify or replace existing affiliate links on any page. Content creators who reviewed products keep their commissions. We only add our own affiliate tag to links you click through our extension. This is a core architectural decision — not a promise, but engineered fact. Our open source code proves it.
What data do you collect?
The bare minimum: the store domain you're on (e.g., "amazon.com") to fetch available coupons. That's it. No browsing history, no personal info, no search queries, no tracking. Everything else (settings, alerts, savings stats) stays on your device in chrome.storage.local. Full privacy policy.
Which stores are supported?
1,000+ stores including Amazon, Walmart, Target, Best Buy, Nike, Adidas, Sephora, Ulta, Etsy, Home Depot, Lowe's, Macy's, Nordstrom, eBay, Chewy, and many more. We're constantly adding new stores.
How is this different from Honey?
Honey was exposed for hijacking affiliate links and hiding the best coupons. Transparent Coupon was built specifically to fix both problems. We never touch existing affiliate links, we show the best deals first, and our Transparency Dashboard shows you exactly what we earn. Our full source code is on GitHub for anyone to audit.
What permissions does the extension need?
storage — saves your settings and alerts locally. tabs — detects which store you're on. clipboardWrite — copies coupon codes. alarms — checks for new coupons periodically. <all_urls> — lets the extension detect stores and show coupon popups. api.gadgethumans.com / api.coupon-partner.com — fetches coupon data.
