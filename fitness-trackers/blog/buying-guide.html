The Complete Fitness Tracker Buying Guide

With so many fitness trackers on the market, finding the right one can feel overwhelming. This guide breaks down everything you need to consider.

What Is a Fitness Tracker?

A fitness tracker is a wearable device that monitors physical activity, heart rate, sleep, and more. From simple step counters to advanced GPS sports watches, there's a tracker for every level of fitness.

Key Features to Consider

Heart Rate Monitoring

Optical heart rate sensors use LEDs to measure blood flow through your skin. Modern sensors are quite accurate for resting and moderate activity, but can lag during high-intensity intervals.

GPS Tracking

Built-in GPS lets you track runs, cycles, and hikes without your phone. Connected GPS uses your phone's GPS — fine for casual use, but less accurate and drains your phone battery.

Sleep Tracking

Most trackers monitor sleep duration, stages (light, deep, REM), and quality. Features like SpO2 (blood oxygen) and respiratory rate add deeper insights.

Battery Life

Fitness bands typically last 5–14 days. GPS watches with colour displays may need charging every 2–7 days. Hybrid watches with analog faces can last weeks.

Which Ecosystem?

Fitbit has the best social features and food logging. Garmin leads in training metrics and GPS accuracy. Polar excels at heart rate science. Xiaomi and Samsung offer great value.

Key Takeaways

  • Casual user? A Fitbit Charge or Xiaomi band is perfect
  • Serious athlete? Go for Garmin or Polar with built-in GPS
  • Battery life matters more than you think
  • Check water resistance if you swim
