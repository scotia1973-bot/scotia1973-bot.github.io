Detailed Breakdown of Each Service

ZeroBounce — $15/mo (2,000 verifications)

ZeroBounce is one of the most well-known names in email verification, and they offer excellent accuracy. But their pricing is shockingly expensive at scale. $15/month gets you only 2,000 verifications — that's $7.50 per 1K. Their free tier is a one-time 100-credit trial. For small SaaS products processing a few thousand signups, you're paying 44x more per verification than necessary.

NeverBounce — $10/mo (1,000 verifications)

NeverBounce is reliable but even pricier than ZeroBounce on a per-verification basis: $10 per 1K verifications. Their free tier is 500 lifetime credits. They do offer bulk list cleaning, but for API-style real-time verification at signup, the cost adds up fast.

Hunter.io — $34/mo (5,000 verifications)

Hunter.io is primarily an email finder tool, but they offer verification as an add-on. At $34/month for 5,000 verifications ($6.80/1K), it's not cheap. If you're already using Hunter for prospecting, the verification might be convenient — but for dedicated signup validation, there are better options.

Clearout — $13/mo (5,000 verifications)

Clearout offers decent pricing at $2.60/1K and good accuracy. Their free tier is a one-time 200 credits. They're a solid mid-tier option, but their paid plans start at $13/month — more than double GadgetHumans' $5 plan for 6x fewer monthly verifications.

EmailListVerify — $9/mo (2,500 verifications)

At $3.60/1K, EmailListVerify is reasonably priced. Their free tier of 500 lifetime credits is generous for testing. However, their API documentation is less developer-friendly, and they lack some advanced features like catch-all detection.

MillionVerifier — $7/mo (5,000 verifications)

MillionVerifier is the closest competitor on price at $1.40/1K. However, their catch-all detection is partial (they mark many valid catch-all domains as "unknown"), and their API response times are slower (avg ~800ms vs GadgetHumans' ~200ms).

QuickEmailVerification — $10/mo (5,000 verifications)

At $2.00/1K, QEV offers decent value. Their API is straightforward and well-documented. But their free tier is only 100 lifetime credits — not enough for meaningful testing — and their paid plans start $5 higher than GadgetHumans for 6x fewer monthly verifications.

Features That Matter Beyond Price

Price isn't everything. Here's what else to consider:

  • SMTP verification: Actually pinging the mail server to confirm the mailbox exists (not all services do this)
  • Disposable email detection: Blocking @mailinator.com, @10minutemail.com, and 10,000+ other temporary domains
  • Catch-all detection: Identifying domains that accept all mail (so you know the verification is inconclusive)
  • API response time: Sub-500ms is critical for real-time signup validation
  • Rate limits: Most services cap you at 5-10 req/sec, even on paid plans

The GadgetHumans API includes all of these features on every tier — including the free plan.
