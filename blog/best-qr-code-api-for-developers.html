Why You Need a Dedicated QR Code API

QR codes are everywhere. Dynamic QR codes (the kind that let you update the destination URL without reprinting) power restaurant menus, event ticketing, inventory tracking, and contactless payments. If you're building any of these into a product, you need an API that:

  • Handles high volume without rate limiting you mid-campaign
  • Generates instantly — sub-100ms response times or bust
  • Offers customization — colors, logos, sizes, and SVG output
  • Won't bankrupt you at scale

How the Others Stack Up

Google Charts QR Code API

Google's QR API is technically free and unlimited, but it's officially deprecated. The endpoint still works (for now), but there's zero support, no SLA, and Google has a history of killing free APIs without warning. If you build your product on this, you're accepting the risk of a 404 page one morning.

goqr.me & qrserver.com

Both are free but severely rate-limited (1-5 requests/second). They also don't support custom colors or SVG output. Fine for generating 10 codes manually. Not fine for any real application.

QR Code Monkey, QRickit, Scanova

These are better suited for marketers than developers. They're priced 2-4x higher than GadgetHumans with lower rate limits. Scanova's $29/mo plan is especially egregious when GadgetHumans offers 10x the volume at 1/6th the price.
